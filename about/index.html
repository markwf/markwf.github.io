About

Jack of all trades, master of some. Operations & strategy professional. Tech startup guy. Content creator.

Born in Germany to British Parents

My parents moved out to Germany before I was born (my dad was offered a job there). They didn't know how to speak German, but they picked it up quickly after enrolling on a 3-month intensive course and they settled down and decided to start a family. I got to spend the first couple years of my life in Bavaria, which I sadly don't remember.

Oct 1992

Moved to England Age Two

When I was two my older brother was coming around to school age so my parents decided to move back to England so he (and I) could grow up with a British education.

Oct 1994

Grew up in a London Suburb

My maternal family all reside in Northampton and my paternal family in Durham. Our family bucked the trend by finding a new home and settling in Hoddesdon, Hertfordshire.

Jan 1995

Joined a Local State School

I grew up in Hoddesdon and joined a local comprehensive called The John Warner School. In my Sixth Form years I specialised in science and mathematics.

Sep 2004

Went to Oxford to Study Biomedical Sciences

I enrolled at the University of Oxford to study Cell and Systems Biology with a plan to pursue a career in medical research.

Oct 2011

Founded Two University Societies

Whilst at university I became increasingly involved with university clubs and societies and founded two of my own: OUSBMS and OUDbC.

Jan 2013

Started Learning to Code

When I launched OUSBMS I needed to make the society a website. Researching the various drag-and-drop solutions I couldn't find anything that did exactly what I wanted (and for free), so I decided to learn how to code and build my own one. I started going to coding meetups run by Oxford Code Lab, Oxford CompSoc and Oxford Entrepreneurs in order to further my skills.

Jan 2013

Change of Career Path

After delving into the world of technology and founding and managing university societies I decided I didn't want to pursue an academic career anymore and instead wanted to pursue a career in tech startups.

May 2013

Furthered Interest in Entrepreneurship

I continued to expand my knowledge in technology and entrepreneurship and joined the Executive Committee of Oxford Entrepreneurs.

Oct 2013

Started Interning at a Healthtech Startup

I postponed my degree and moved to London to learn more about the tech startup scene. I got an intern position at a Westminster-based healthtech startup where I started gaining professional experience.

Sep 2014

Joined a Coding Bootcamp

I wanted to improve on my tech skills and so applied for a place at the Founders & Coders coding bootcamp. I attended full-time for 2 months in which time I learned more about JavaScript Web Development.

Jan 2015

Completed Degree

Following the bootcamp I returned to Oxford to complete my undergraduate degree over the summer of 2015.

Mar 2015

Joined Entrepreneur First as Operations Manager

After completing my degree I decided to join Europe's top seed-stage venture capitalist firm and deep technology startup accelerator, Entrepreneur First. EF helps the world's top technical talent start deep, defensible technology companies. In this role I learnt a lot about early-stage startups, investment and legals.

Sep 2015

Joined Hack Partners as Head of Operations

I was offered a role at Hack Partners, an innovation consultancy firm that is specialising in disrupting heavily-regulated and technologically-underdeveloped industries. HP work mainly with the railway industry, operating under the well-known HackTrain brand. Amongst the daily business operations, I managed and organised the HackTrain Hackathon, as well as the annual HackTrain Conference and the HackTrain Accelerator, the world's first programme of its kind specifically designed to meet the demands of the growing RailTech movement.

Apr 2016

Promoted to Founding Partner at Hack Partners

Within a few months of working at Hack Partners I was promoted to Founding Partner status. The position came with increased equity and more responsibility within the business.

Jul 2016

Joined DWYL as Chief Operating Officer

At the start of 2017 I decided to make the move to DWYL - a digital agency that works with social enterprises and charities to create products and technical tools in order to foster valuable social change through the use of digital technology. DWYL builds apps and tools that empower businesses and individuals.

Jan 2017

What's Next?

After spending a year at DWYL I'm now moving on to new challenges. If you have one that might be of interest then check out my full CV and be sure to send an email over.
